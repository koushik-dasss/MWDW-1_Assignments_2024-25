Cuttack

Cuttack, one of Odisha’s oldest cities, is a perfect blend of history, culture, and vibrant traditions. Known as the “Millennium City” and the “Silver City,” Cuttack boasts a rich heritage that is evident in its historic landmarks, like the Barabati Fort, a 14th-century structure that stands as a reminder of the city’s glorious past. The city is also home to the Netaji Birthplace Museum, preserving the legacy of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, one of India’s greatest freedom fighters.

Cuttack’s culture comes alive during its famous festivals like Durga Puja, celebrated with grand pandals and intricate silver filigree work, an art for which the city is renowned. The traditional craft of silver filigree is deeply embedded in the city’s identity, making it a must-see for visitors.

The culinary landscape of Cuttack is equally delightful, with dishes like the famous Dahibara Aloodum, a spicy yogurt-based snack, being a local favorite. Street food vendors line the city streets, offering an authentic taste of Odisha.
